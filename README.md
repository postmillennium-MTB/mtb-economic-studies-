# mtb-economic-studies-
ROI for building trails 
please review the below economic studies below  that derive connection from spending money on trails and that money paying off in the form of economic benefit to community and tax revenue.  in other words, there is an ROI to spending money building and maintaining trails. 
task:  please create an interactive widget in HTML that can be used to adjust assumptions and calculate break even for a given municipality. please include a breakeven graph and other charts or visuals that could be helpful.   Please include optional  factors like maintenance - which for trails is often $1,500-$5,000 per mile per year.  when possible, differentiate direct revenue and indirect revenue.  please include ability to switch between "conservative" "moderate" and "optimistic" scenarios
the idea i'm trying to convey here is that when a town invests in building new trails, that is not a "sunken cost" it comes back in many ways.  if possible, please include a metric related to home values.  feel free to do additional research beyond the links below that connect single track MTB trails to economic benefits.  
i'd like this interactive tool to be useful for people wanting to advocate for budding trails in their community as well as decision makers (such as city council) to make well informed decisions based on real data.  
for a point of  comparison, include a metric for how much it cost to build a mile of highway.
