Data sources & methodology:
Visitor spending benchmarks from Trust for Public Land / IMBA Economic Benefits of Mountain Biking (2025) — meta-analysis of 50 studies. Conservative ($103/trip) based on Duluth, MN data; moderate ($175/trip) is a regional mid-range; optimistic ($416/trip) is the TPL 2025 national average. Property value premiums from Crompton & Nicholls (2019) review of 20+ trail studies; NAR Field Guide on Trails and Property Values. Trail build costs: Singletracks.com contractor survey (April 2026), American Trails database. Maintenance costs: American Trails $1,500–$5,000/mile/year range. Highway costs: FHWA, Quora/DOT data (2020–2024). Job estimates use $45,000 labor income per FTE from IMBA regional studies. Economic multiplier of 1.2–1.8× is standard for rural/small-city tourism analysis.

Definitions: Direct / municipal revenue = sales tax captured by municipality + property tax from home value premium. Economic impact = total visitor spending in local economy, adjusted for overnight share and multiplier. Breakeven = year when cumulative municipal revenue first exceeds initial construction cost (maintenance excluded). This tool is for planning and advocacy — validate with local tax rate and visitor data for formal municipal presentations.
