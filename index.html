Data sources & methodology:
Visitor spending: TPL/IMBA Economic Benefits of Mountain Biking (2025), meta-analysis of 50 studies. Conservative ($103/trip) = Duluth, MN data; moderate ($175/trip) = regional average; optimistic ($416/trip) = TPL national average. Property value premiums: Crompton & Nicholls (2019) review of 20+ trail studies; NAR Field Guide on Trails and Property Values. Trail build costs: Singletracks.com contractor survey (April 2026). Highway costs: FHWA, DOT data (2020–2024). Jobs: IMBA benchmark ~$45K labor income per FTE.

Modeling notes (new in this version): Visitor ramp-up — new trails typically reach 40% of target capacity in Year 1, growing linearly to 100% over the ramp-up period (based on Duluth, Tasmania, and Kingdom Trails case studies). Annual visitor growth — applied after ramp-up; MTB market growing 5.8–8.9% CAGR globally (Technavio 2025); local trail growth is typically 2–5%. Maintenance inflation — trail maintenance is labor-intensive; maintenance costs escalate at the selected CPI rate each year. At 3% inflation, Year 20 maintenance is ~81% higher in nominal terms than Year 1. Local vs. out-of-town visitors — out-of-town visitors bring new money to the local economy and receive the full economic multiplier; local visitors recirculate existing spending (multiplier = 1.0), per methodology in Duluth (2017) and Buning & Lamont (2021) critical review of 33 MTB economic impact studies.

Definitions: Breakeven = year cumulative municipal revenue (sales tax + property tax) first exceeds construction cost (maintenance excluded from breakeven calc). Economic impact = out-of-town visitor spending × multiplier + local visitor spending. This tool is for planning and advocacy — validate with local tax rate and visitor data for formal presentations.
